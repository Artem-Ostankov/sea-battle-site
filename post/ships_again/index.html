Ships Again

procrastination rules

A 2 minute read, Posted on Wed, Apr 12, 2017

I’ve spent last few days braking my head against the wall.

**Literally ! **

Game design appeared to be really hard stuff …

Well, you see, by now I tried many mechanics prototypes to figure out what makes more fun. And the answer is still : ** I have no damn idea ** Playthrough with real people didn’t help much so far, as all the possibilities looks more or less interesting but none is really finite. By finite I mean that mechanics will be intuitive and interesting enough by itself without much of gamification.

Those are some ideas I tried to iterate through: - floating islands - capturing islands - color gatherers, mixers and projectors, where each color would yield special action - ships where each cell has special function - etc. And these all needs to seat on top of WeGo mechanics…

The problem with all of them is that it becomes complicated very fast. Complicated for player and for development.

So I was constantly saying the mantra: “keep it simple, keep it simple, keep it … f*ck, how ?!! “ Very important for me was to stay within these boundaries: 1. Simple to start and hard to master. 2. Depth from mechanics, not from features. 3. Replayability (so you can’t just repeat the same strategy every time).

So several days I was really trying to put a fat dot and stop with prototypes to decide which mechanics to use. Yo, It wasn’t easy. But somehow few days of hard thinking and few more days of procrastination yielded the result.

We come back to ships ! :) Yes, at first glance it will look like an old school pen&paper Sea Battle. And this is huge advantage, as people will directly grasp the goal and basic mechanics.

Also, I will not over stress them with complicated mixture of modules in the begining. Each ship will have its function determined by the size of ship. So it’s very easy to see and intuitive: big ship = big Boom :)

And on top an intuitive base, we put WeGo and “Action Cards” so player will start slowly master the game and will get the idea how he can play better by utilizing the hints and thinking tactically.

Of course, it might not be the ultimate right mechanics, but I have a good feeling and this approach fits my boundaries very well. And I feel finally relived, as I can concentrate on implementation and not changing core mechanics too much anymore. So let’s see what would come out!
